Paneer Tikka

Ingredients

  • 250 - 300g paneer, cut into thick, large cubes
  • 1 large onion, cut into 1-inch squares and layers separated
  • 1 large green or colored bell pepper, cut into 1-inch squares
  • 1/2 cup hung curd
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste1 tbsp mustard oil
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi, crushed
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • 1 tbsp roasted gram flour
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Whisk the Marinade: In a large mixing bowl, whisk the hung curd until it is completely smooth and free of lumps.
  2. Add Spices: Add the ginger-garlic paste, mustard oil, lemon juice, salt, and all the dry spice.
    3. powders to the curd. Mix thoroughly until the marinade is uniform and vibrant.
  3. Coat the Ingredients: Gently fold the paneer cubes, onion squares, and bell pepper pieces into the marinade. Ensure all pieces are evenly coated without breaking the paneer.
  4. Marinate: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for a minimum of 2 hours.
  5. Skewer: If using wooden bamboo skewers, soak them in water for 15–20 minutes so they do not burn. Thread the marinated paneer, onions, and bell peppers alternately onto the skewers.
  6. Cook: Heat a grill pan or heavy tawa over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil. Place the skewers on the pan and cook for 2–3 minutes. Rotate the skewers, brush with a little oil or butter, and cook until all sides are nicely charred and golden brown.
  7. Serve: Carefully remove the tikka from the skewers onto a serving plate. Sprinkle with chaat masala and serve hot with mint-coriander chutney.
